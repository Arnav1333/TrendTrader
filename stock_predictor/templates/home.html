Stock Market Terminologies

Understanding stock market terminology is crucial for making informed investment decisions. Here are some essential terms:

Bull Market

A market condition where prices are rising or expected to rise, indicating investor confidence.

Bear Market

A market condition where prices are falling or expected to fall, indicating investor pessimism.

Market Capitalization

The total value of a company's shares in the market, calculated as stock price multiplied by total shares.

P/E Ratio (Price to Earnings Ratio)

A valuation metric that shows the relationship between a company's stock price and its earnings per share.

Dividend

A portion of a company's profits distributed to shareholders as a reward for holding shares.

Liquidity

The ease with which an asset or security can be converted into cash without affecting its market price.

📈 Why Integrate GBM with LSTM for Stock Prediction?

1️⃣ Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM): GBM is a stochastic process used to simulate stock prices based on randomness and historical trends. However, it assumes market efficiency and lacks deep pattern recognition.

2️⃣ Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM): LSTMs are powerful neural networks that capture long-term dependencies in sequential data, making them ideal for recognizing stock market patterns.

🔥 Synergy of GBM & LSTM:

  • GBM provides a probabilistic baseline for stock movements based on volatility.
  • LSTM fine-tunes predictions by learning complex, non-linear market trends.
  • Combining both enhances accuracy, reducing reliance on single-method forecasting.

📊 Practical Example:

GBM predicts potential price movement ranges, while LSTM refines predictions using time-series patterns, sentiment, and momentum.

🚀 This hybrid approach is widely used in AI-driven trading and quantitative finance!
